# Design System Inspired by Moore

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

Moore embodies a sophisticated, minimalist aesthetic designed for ambitious brands seeking strategic communication and branding solutions. The design system emphasizes clarity, professionalism, and purposeful restraint, combining deep navy foundations with warm neutral tones to create an atmosphere of trustworthiness and forward-thinking innovation. The visual language prioritizes whitespace and refined typography, allowing content to breathe while maintaining a contemporary, boutique-agency sensibility. Every element serves a communicative purpose, reflecting the brand's commitment to elevating storytelling and brand impact on a global stage.

**Key Characteristics**
- Minimalist, purposeful layout with generous whitespace
- Deep navy primary color paired with warm cream and beige neutrals
- Clean, modern typography hierarchy using Inter Display
- Professional yet approachable tone
- Strong focus on content over decoration
- Refined, luxury-adjacent aesthetic suitable for consultancies and professional services

| Level | Treatment | Use |

**Shadow Philosophy:** Moore employs minimal, subtle shadows to maintain its minimalist aesthetic. Shadows are used sparingly to denote interactive hierarchy rather than create dramatic depth. A flat, borderline approach is preferred; when shadows are applied, they are soft and restrained. The primary navy (`#1B2D73`) is used at low opacity for focus states, creating a visual connection to brand color.

## 7. Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- **Do** use the deep navy (`#1B2D73`) as the primary color for all CTAs, headings, and interactive elements
- **Do** maintain consistent `0px` border radius across all buttons, inputs, and cards for architectural precision
- **Do** pair Inter Display at 600-weight for all headlines and 500-weight for body and links
- **Do** apply generous padding (`48px–64px`) to major sections for breathing room and visual clarity
- **Do** use the warm off-white background (`#FAF8F2`) as the default page background for a subtle, sophisticated tone
- **Do** employ consistent `60px–64px` vertical spacing between full-width sections
- **Do** reserve the sky blue accent (`#9DC4FF`) for hover states, active indicators, or secondary emphasis
- **Do** center all content within a `1200px` max-width container with `48px` side padding
- **Do** combine text and imagery at large scales to communicate brand authority and expertise
- **Do** use the slate gray (`#646670`) for secondary body text and metadata

### Don't

- **Don't** use heavily rounded corners (border-radius > 3px) on primary UI elements; maintain the minimalist, architectural aesthetic
- **Don't** apply multiple shadows to a single element; keep elevation subtle and single-layered
- **Don't** mix font weights excessively; use 600 px–32px`): Section gaps, list spacing, navigation margins
- Large (`40px–56px`): Section separation, grid gaps, breathing room
- Extra-Large (`60px–64px`): Hero sections, major layout shifts, full-width container padding

### Grid & Container

- **Max Width:** `100%` (full-bleed sections); individual content areas constrain to `1200px` maximum
- **Column Strategy:** 12-column flexible grid on desktop; collapses to 6-column on tablet, single-column on mobile
- **Gutter Width:** `32px` (16px per side) between columns
- **Section Padding:** `64px` vertical, `48px` horizontal on desktop
- **Content Padding Mobile:** `20px` horizontal, `40px` vertical
- **Aspect Ratio Consistency:** Image containers maintain 16:9 or 4:3 ratios; hero images fill full viewport height on desktop, capped at `600px` on mobile

### Whitespace Philosophy

Moore employs whitespace as a primary design element, not a residual gap. Every section breathes; content never feels crowded. The design separates form from decoration through negative space rather than visual noise. Large, generous margins between sections allow the eye to rest and guide focus sequentially through the narrative. Whitespace communicates confidence and intentionality—a lawyer's office with high ceilings and marble floors, not a cluttered back office.

### Border Radius Scale

- `0px` – All primary UI elements (buttons, inputs, cards, containers). Sharp corners enforce the clean, professional aesthetic.
- `3px` – Avatar images, small inline icons, and subtle badge elements where minimal softness aids visual distinction.
- `2px` – Checkbox and radio button borders for micro refinement.

*No element uses rounded corners greater than `3px`; the system maintains a geometric, contemporary feel.*

## 7. Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- **Use navy (`#1B2D73`) as the dominant text and interaction color** to maintain brand consistency and establish trust through repetition.
- **Leverage generous whitespace** (`40px–64px` gaps) between sections to create visual breathing room and guide sequential content consumption.
- **Apply Inter Display at weights 600 (headings) and 500 (body) exclusively** to maintain typographic cohesion across all interfaces.
- **Implement sharp borders (`0px` radius) on buttons, inputs, and cards** to reinforce the geometric, contemporary aesthetic.
- **Use the light neutral palette (`#FAF8F2`, `#ECEAE5`) for backgrounds** to reduce cognitive strain and support long-form reading.
- **Reserve `#9DC4FF` accent color for interactive states and subtle emphasis only**, never as a dominant surface color.
- **Align form inputs and buttons to a `12px` minimum padding baseline** for touch-friendly mobile experiences.
- **Apply subtle shadows (`Level 1–2` only) to cards and containers** to indicate elevation without overwhelming the interface.
- **Group related content within cards or sections with consistent `48px` padding** to create cognitive boundaries.
- **Maintain consistent line heights (1.1–1.5x font size)** across all typography for readability and visual rhythm.

### Don't

- **Never mix typefaces or fall back to system fonts;** always render Inter Display, with the specified fallback stack only if necessary.
- **Avoid rounded corners greater than `3px`**; the system's geometric character depends on sharp and minimal radius values.
- **Don't use more than four colors in a single section** (navy, accent blue, warm cream, and neutral gray are sufficient).
- **Never apply shadow levels greater than `Level 2` outside of modals or critical interactive states;** over-shadowing suggests design uncertainty.
- **Avoid tight spacing (`< 16px`) between major sections;** let content breathe with a minimum `40px` gap.
- **Don't justify-align body text;** use left-align for legibility and consistent word spacing.
- **Never use `#9DC4FF` as a text color on light backgrounds** (use navy `#1B2D73` instead for contrast compliance).
- **Avoid combining bold weight headings with all-caps text;** the system prioritizes elegant hierarchy, not shouting.
- **Don't apply hover effects to non-interactive elements** (images, decorative elements); reserve transitions for buttons, links, and form states.
- **Never disable form validation feedback;** always indicate error states with clear visual cues and explanatory text.
